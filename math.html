Unit 1 Content

Mathematics-III (BCA-4005)

1. Complex Number System

Definition: A number of the form $z = x + iy$, where $x, y \in \mathbb{R}$ and $i = \sqrt{-1}$.

  • Real Part: $Re(z) = x$
  • Imaginary Part: $Im(z) = y$
  • Conjugate ($\bar{z}$): If $z = x + iy$, then $\bar{z} = x - iy$.
  • Modulus ($|z|$): Distance from origin. $|z| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$. PYQ 2022
  • Powers of $i$: Cycle of 4 ($i, -1, -i, 1$).
    Example: $i^{19} = i^{4(4)+3} = i^3 = -i$ PYQ 2024

2. Polar Form & Arguments

Representing $z$ using radius $r$ and angle $\theta$.

Form: $z = r(\cos\theta + i\sin\theta)$ or $z = re^{i\theta}$ (Euler's Form).

  • $r = |z|$ (Modulus)
  • $\theta = \tan^{-1}(y/x)$ (Argument or Amplitude).
  • Principal Argument: Value of $\theta$ in $(-\pi, \pi]$.

3. De Moivre's Theorem PYQ 2023

Theorem: For any real number $n$, $(\cos\theta + i\sin\theta)^n = \cos(n\theta) + i\sin(n\theta)$.

Use: Finding powers and roots of complex numbers.

Roots of Unity: Solutions to $z^n = 1$.
Cube roots of unity ($1, \omega, \omega^2$): sum $1+\omega+\omega^2=0$, product $\omega^3=1$. PYQ 2023

  • De Moivre's Theorem: $(\cos\theta + i\sin\theta)^n = \cos(n\theta) + i\sin(n\theta)$.
  • Roots of Unity ($1^{1/3}$): The cube roots of unity are $1, \omega, \omega^2$.
    • Properties: $1 + \omega + \omega^2 = 0$ and $\omega^3 = 1$.
  • Analytic Functions: A function $f(z)$ differentiable at every point in a domain.
    • Cauchy-Riemann (C-R) Equations: Necessary condition for differentiability.
      $\frac{\partial u}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial v}{\partial y}$ and $\frac{\partial u}{\partial y} = -\frac{\partial v}{\partial x}$.

4. Functions of Complex Variable

Function $f(z)$: A rule assigning a complex number $w$ to $z$. $w = f(z) = u(x,y) + iv(x,y)$.

  • Limit: $lim_{z \to z_0} f(z) = L$. Path independent approach required.
  • Continuity: Limit exists and equals function value.
  • Differentiability: Derivative exists. A function differentiable everywhere is Analytic (Holomorphic). Verified using Cauchy-Riemann Equations ($u_x=v_y, u_y=-v_x$). PYQ 2024

Unit 2 Content

Mathematics-III

Vector Calculus

  • Gradient ($\nabla \phi$): Acts on a Scalar function $\phi$. Result is a Vector. Represents normal to surface.
    Physical Meaning: Represents the rate of change of a scalar field. Direction of $\nabla \phi$ gives the direction of steepest ascent.
    $\nabla \phi = \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial x}\hat{i} + \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial y}\hat{j} + \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial z}\hat{k}$.
  • Divergence ($\nabla \cdot \vec{F}$): Dot product of $\nabla$ and Vector $\vec{F}$. Result is Scalar.
    Physical Meaning: Represents the rate of flux expansion (source) or contraction (sink) per unit volume.
    If $\text{div } \vec{F} = 0$, vector is Solenoidal (incompressible flow).
  • Curl ($\nabla \times \vec{F}$): Cross product. Result is Vector.
    Physical Meaning: Represents the rotation or angular velocity of a vector field.
    If $\text{curl } \vec{F} = 0$, vector is Irrotational (conservative field).
  • Directional Derivative: Rate of change of $\phi$ in direction of vector $\vec{a}$.
    Formula: D.D. = $\nabla \phi \cdot \frac{\vec{a}}{|\vec{a}|}$ (Grad $\phi$ dot unit vector).

Unit 3 Content

Mathematics-III

Fourier Series

  • Definition: Expression of a periodic function $f(x)$ as a sum of sines and cosines.
  • Formula: $f(x) = \frac{a_0}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n \cos(nx) + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n \sin(nx)$.
  • Euler's Formulas:
    • $a_0 = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) dx$
    • $a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) \cos(nx) dx$
    • $b_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) \sin(nx) dx$
  • Even Function ($f(-x) = f(x)$): Symmetric about Y-axis. $b_n = 0$.
  • Odd Function ($f(-x) = -f(x)$): Symmetric about Origin. $a_0 = 0, a_n = 0$, only sine terms exist.
  • Dirichlet Conditions: Conditions for a function $f(x)$ to be represented as a Fourier Series:
    1. Defined and single-valued.
    2. Finite number of discontinuities in period.
    3. Finite number of maxima and minima.
    4. $\int_{-\pi}^{\pi} |f(x)| dx$ (Absolute integral) converges.

Unit 4 Content

Mathematics-III

Ordinary Differential Equations (First Order)

  • Order vs. Degree: Order = Highest derivative. Degree = Power of highest derivative.
  • Variable Separable: Separate $x$ and $y$ terms and integrate.
  • Linear ODE: Form $\frac{dy}{dx} + Py = Q$.
    • Integrating Factor (IF) = $e^{\int P dx}$. Solution: $y(IF) = \int (Q \cdot IF) dx + C$.
  • Homogeneous Equation: Degree of terms in $x$ and $y$ is same. Put $y=vx$.
  • Bernoulli’s Equation: form $\frac{dy}{dx} + Py = Qy^n$. Solution: Divide by $y^n$, put $v = y^{1-n}$ to make it Linear.
  • Exact Differential Equation: $Mdx + Ndy = 0$ is exact if $\frac{\partial M}{\partial y} = \frac{\partial N}{\partial x}$.
    Solution: $\int M dx (\text{y const}) + \int (\text{terms of N free from x}) dy = C$.
  • Orthogonal Trajectories: Curves cutting a family of curves at $90^\circ$.
    Method: Form differential equation. Replace $\frac{dy}{dx}$ with $-\frac{dx}{dy}$. Solve new DE.

Mathematics-III

ODE of Second Order

  • General Form: $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} + P\frac{dy}{dx} + Qy = R(x)$.
  • Solution ($y$): Complementary Function (C.F.) + Particular Integral (P.I.).
  • Finding C.F. (Auxiliary Equation $m^2+Pm+Q=0$):
    • Real & Distinct ($m_1, m_2$): $y = c_1 e^{m_1x} + c_2 e^{m_2x}$.
    • Real & Equal ($m, m$): $y = (c_1 + c_2x)e^{mx}$.
    • Complex ($\alpha \pm i\beta$): $y = e^{\alpha x}(c_1 \cos \beta x + c_2 \sin \beta x)$.
  • Finding P.I.:
    • If $R(x) = e^{ax}$, P.I. = $\frac{e^{ax}}{f(a)}$ (provided $f(a) \neq 0$).
    • Case of Failure: Multiply by $x$ and differentiate denominator.
  • Laplace Transform: Converts time-domain functions $f(t)$ to s-domain $F(s)$.
    • $L\{1\} = 1/s$
    • $L\{t^n\} = n!/s^{n+1}$
    • $L\{e^{at}\} = 1/(s-a)$
    • $L\{\sin at\} = a/(s^2+a^2)$
